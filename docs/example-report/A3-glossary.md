# Appendix A3: Glossary

## Business and Financial Terms

**ACV (Annual Contract Value)**
: The normalized annual revenue from a customer contract. For multi-year deals, total contract value divided by number of years.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**
: Predictable and recurring revenue components of business, normalized to one calendar year. Excludes one-time fees.

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
: Total revenue divided by number of users/customers. Metric for per-customer revenue generation.

**Break-even Point**
: The point at which total revenue equals total costs, resulting in neither profit nor loss. For this report, achieved Q2 2025.

**Burn Rate**
: The rate at which a company spends its cash reserves, typically measured monthly. Critical metric for runway calculation.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
: Total sales and marketing expenses divided by number of new customers acquired in the period.

**CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)**
: The mean annual growth rate over a specified period longer than one year. Formula: (End Value / Start Value)^(1/Years) - 1.

**Churn Rate**
: Percentage of customers who discontinue service during a given period. Calculated as: (Customers Lost / Total Customers at Start) × 100.

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization)**
: Measure of operating performance that excludes effects of financing and accounting decisions.

**Gross Margin**
: Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of revenue. Indicates pricing power and operational efficiency.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**
: Discount rate that makes net present value of cash flows equal to zero. Used to evaluate investment attractiveness.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
: Total revenue expected from a customer over the entire relationship. Formula: ARPU × (1 / Churn Rate) × Gross Margin.

**LTV/CAC Ratio**
: Lifetime value divided by customer acquisition cost. Ratio > 3.0 generally considered healthy for SaaS businesses.

**MAU (Monthly Active Users)**
: Number of unique users who engage with product in a calendar month. Key engagement metric.

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**
: Predictable monthly revenue from subscriptions. Equivalent to ARR / 12.

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
: Customer loyalty metric. Percentage of promoters (9-10 rating) minus percentage of detractors (0-6 rating). Range: -100 to +100.

**Payback Period**
: Time required to recover customer acquisition cost through gross profit. Calculated as: CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin).

**ROI (Return on Investment)**
: Gain from investment relative to its cost. Formula: (Current Value - Investment Cost) / Investment Cost × 100.

**Rule of 40**
: SaaS metric stating that growth rate plus profit margin should exceed 40%. Balances growth and profitability.

**Runway**
: Number of months company can operate before running out of cash, assuming current burn rate and no additional funding.

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**
: Cloud-based software delivery model where applications are licensed on subscription basis and centrally hosted.

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
: Total market demand for a product or service, representing maximum revenue opportunity if 100% market share achieved.

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**
: Portion of TAM targeted by products and services, within geographic reach.

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**
: Portion of SAM realistically achievable in near term, accounting for competition and constraints.

**TCV (Total Contract Value)**
: Total value of contract including one-time and recurring fees, without normalization to annual period.

**Unit Economics**
: Revenues and costs associated with a business model expressed on a per-unit basis. Key indicator of scalability.

## Technical Terms

**CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment)**
: Software development practice of automatically testing and deploying code changes to production.

**Docker**
: Platform for developing, shipping, and running applications in containers. Provides consistent environment across development and production.

**ECharts**
: Open-source JavaScript visualization library. Used in this template for creating interactive charts.

**Markdown**
: Lightweight markup language with plain text formatting syntax. Source format for this documentation.

**Microservices**
: Architectural style structuring application as collection of loosely coupled services. Enables independent scaling and deployment.

**MTTR (Mean Time To Recovery)**
: Average time required to recover from system failure. Key reliability metric.

**Pandoc**
: Universal document converter. Used in this template to convert markdown to PDF via LaTeX.

**Puppeteer**
: Node.js library providing API to control headless Chrome browser. Used for rendering charts to images.

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)**
: Commitment between service provider and client defining service quality expectations and metrics.

**VitePress**
: Static site generator powered by Vite and Vue. Used in this template for documentation websites.

**XeLaTeX**
: TeX typesetting engine with modern features like Unicode support and OpenType fonts. Used for PDF generation.

## Market and Industry Terms

**B2B (Business-to-Business)**
: Transactions between businesses, rather than between business and individual consumer.

**Enterprise**
: Large organizations typically with >1000 employees. Target market segment with higher ACV.

**Go-to-Market (GTM)**
: Strategy for delivering product to end customers, encompassing sales, marketing, and distribution.

**Mid-Market**
: Organizations with 100-1000 employees. Balance between enterprise and SMB segments.

**Positioning**
: How product is perceived in target market relative to competitors. Defines unique value proposition.

**Segment**
: Distinct group of customers with similar characteristics, needs, or behaviors.

**SMB (Small and Medium Business)**
: Organizations with <100 employees. Often price-sensitive with simpler requirements.

**Vertical**
: Specific industry or market segment (e.g., healthcare, finance, manufacturing).

## Risk Management Terms

**Impact**
: Magnitude of consequence if risk materializes. Measured on scale (e.g., 1-10) considering financial, operational, and strategic effects.

**Mitigation**
: Actions taken to reduce probability or impact of risk. Part of risk response strategy.

**Probability**
: Likelihood of risk event occurring. Estimated based on historical data, expert judgment, and environmental factors.

**Risk Appetite**
: Amount and type of risk organization is willing to accept in pursuit of objectives.

**Risk Matrix**
: Visual tool plotting risks by probability and impact. Used to prioritize risk response efforts.

**Risk Score**
: Quantitative measure of risk, typically calculated as Probability × Impact. Used for prioritization.

**Risk Register**
: Document recording identified risks, their assessment, and planned response strategies.

## Statistical and Analytical Terms

**Benchmark**
: Standard or point of reference against which things can be compared. Used to assess relative performance.

**Correlation**
: Statistical relationship between two variables. Does not imply causation.

**Forecast**
: Prediction of future conditions based on analysis of historical data and trends.

**Hypothesis**
: Proposed explanation for phenomenon, used as starting point for investigation or analysis.

**Median**
: Middle value in dataset when arranged in order. Less sensitive to outliers than mean.

**Outlier**
: Data point significantly different from other observations. May indicate error or unusual event.

**Percentile**
: Value below which given percentage of observations fall. E.g., 75th percentile means 75% of data points are below this value.

**Trend**
: General direction of data over time. Can be upward, downward, or stable.

**Validation**
: Process of checking data or methodology for accuracy and appropriateness.

**Variance**
: Measure of spread in dataset. Square of standard deviation.

## Document Structure Terms

**Appendix**
: Supplementary material at end of document providing additional detail without disrupting main narrative flow.

**Chapter**
: Major division of document covering distinct topic or theme. Numbered sequentially (1, 2, 3...).

**Cross-reference**
: Link or pointer to related content elsewhere in document. Enables non-linear reading.

**Executive Summary**
: Brief overview of document highlighting key findings and recommendations. Designed for senior stakeholders.

**Frontmatter**
: Metadata at beginning of markdown file. Includes title, description, and other properties.

**Heading**
: Title of section or subsection. Hierarchical structure (H1, H2, H3...) organizes content.

**Table of Contents (TOC)**
: List of chapter and section headings with page numbers. Provides document overview and navigation aid.

## Template-Specific Terms

**Chart ID**
: Unique identifier for chart. Can be explicitly specified via HTML comment or auto-generated from content hash.

**Docker Container**
: Isolated environment containing chart renderer and all dependencies. Ensures consistent rendering across systems.

**Image Manifest**
: JSON file listing all charts extracted from markdown, with their IDs, source files, and configurations.

**Preprocessing**
: Step where chart code blocks are replaced with image references before PDF generation.

**Theme**
: Collection of styles, colors, and layout settings defining visual appearance of VitePress site.
